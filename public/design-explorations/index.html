Tunixlabs · Design Explorations · 2026-04

Three distinct aesthetic
directions. Pick one.

Each direction pulls a different principle from jeskojets.com and lands at a distant point in design space. None is "the answer" — pick the one whose personality matches how you want to be perceived by a mining director, a tax auditor, or a US nearshore buyer. Open each in its own tab and compare side-by-side.

Direction A
Editorial Monolith
Dark cinematic · serif display

Warm browns and deep black. Heavy display serif (Fraunces variable) with italic Instrument Serif dek. Full-bleed atmospheric gradients, editorial single-column rhythm, grain overlay. Feels like a long-read magazine profile.

Principle from ref
→ Invisible luxury, dramatic reveals
Personality
→ Trustworthy, considered, premium
Direction B
Industrial Blueprint
Technical drawing · mono + plex sans

Engineering drawing aesthetic applied to software. Graph paper grid, dimensional callouts, BOM-style service schedule, oxidized copper accents, constructive line draws. Feels like reading a mech drawing — but for software.

Principle from ref
→ Technical sophistication (the blueprint schematic)
Personality
→ Engineer-to-engineer, zero bullshit
Direction C
Kinetic Swiss
Absurd type · acid accent

Massive Archivo Black display at 17vw. Pure ink/paper palette with acid lime accent. Text-as-image hero, horizontal marquee, asymmetric 12-col service grid with full-card invert hover. Feels like a modern studio owns the room.

Principle from ref
→ Motion with purpose, asymmetric grid
Personality
→ Confident, modern, unapologetic
How to compare: open each in a new tab, scroll the hero, hover the service cards (each has a different hover treatment), and notice the typography contrast. The reference jeskojets.com has more principles than three — these are the three I think fit the "solo operator · regulated industries · honest" brief. If none feel right, say which element of which one you want to steal and I can build a hybrid.
