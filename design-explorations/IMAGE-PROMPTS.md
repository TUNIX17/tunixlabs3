# Gemini 3 Pro Image Prompts · Tunixlabs Kinetic Swiss v2

**Objetivo:** 5 imágenes con tratamiento visual coherente para el redesign de tunixlabs.com.

**Estilo visual compartido (ninguna imagen debería escapar de esto):**
- Photography de editorial magazine, no stock, no AI slop
- Desaturated palette: 25-35% de la saturación original, hacia tonos azul-noche o cobre-amanecer
- Side-light desde la derecha, sombras profundas a la izquierda
- Film grain sutil (400-800 ISO look)
- Radial vignette muy suave en las esquinas
- Composiciones asimétricas con negative space en un lado (para que el texto del diseño respire)
- Sin stock-photo happy faces, sin gente mirando a cámara sonriendo, sin manos diversas sobre laptop
- Siempre contexto industrial real chileno/latinoamericano

**Paleta objetivo:** la imagen debe poder vivir sobre fondo `#f5f5f2` (paper) sin chocar. Evitar colores saturados fuera de cobre `#b85c38` y azul-noche `#1a2540`.

**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 por default, excepto donde se indique.

## Image 01 · Hero background — Operación minera nocturna

**Uso:** fondo del hero, dentro del terminal window, desaturada y blurreada 2px detrás del texto Archivo Black masivo.

**Prompt para /imagine:**

```
Wide cinematic shot of an open-pit copper mining operation in northern Chile at
blue hour, 30 minutes after sunset. Foreground: the edge of a massive haul truck
tire (partial view, bottom-left of frame, in deep shadow). Mid-ground: three
mining workers in orange high-visibility vests and white hard hats walking
between industrial equipment, their backs to camera, small against the scale of
machinery. Background: the terraced walls of the open pit receding into darkness,
illuminated by distant sodium-vapor work lights creating warm amber pinpoints.
Sky: deep indigo blue fading to muted copper-orange at the horizon line.

Composition: strong negative space in the upper-right two-thirds of the frame
(for text overlay). The human subjects anchor the bottom-left third. Rule of
thirds respected. Wide angle, 24mm equivalent, cinematic 2.39:1 crop.

Lighting: side-light from the right (warm sodium lamps), deep cool shadows from
the left. High contrast but preserved shadow detail. Grain of ISO 1600 pushed
film. Color grading desaturated to 30% with cooled-blue shadows and warm-copper
highlights (classic orange-and-teal cinematography but pulled back, not
aggressive).

Atmosphere: dust hanging in the air catching the edge light, giving volumetric
haze. Distant work lights creating soft bokeh points.

Mood: serious, real, documentary. Not heroic, not advertising. A worker's end
of shift, not a marketing visual.

Avoid: stock photo smiles, people looking at camera, saturated colors, fake
lens flares, modern clean aesthetic, startup vibe, corporate polish, any text
or logos on equipment, generic "Chinese mining" look.

Style reference: Edward Burtynsky industrial landscape photography, but with
tighter human scale. Cinematography reference: "Sicario" (2015) color grading
for the border crossing scenes.

Resolution: 2560x1440. Format: photographic, not illustrated.
```

## Image 02 · Case study Schwager — Operario con Voice AI en faena

**Uso:** case study card principal, hover reveal dentro del terminal. Foto del producto Voice AI en acción.

**Prompt para /imagine:**

```
Medium close-up of a mining field worker in his 40s, wearing a white hard hat
with reflective stripes and thick leather work gloves, holding a rugged
smartphone up to his ear in a speaking gesture. He is mid-sentence, mouth
slightly open, concentrated expression — NOT smiling at camera, NOT posing.
Three-quarter profile view, his face turned slightly away. Background slightly
out of focus: a maintenance workshop in a Chilean mining operation at dusk,
with red metal structures and a hint of machinery suggesting a haul truck
service bay.

The phone in his hand shows a subtle green circular waveform on its screen
(Voice AI listening indicator) — barely visible but present, camera can detect
it.

Composition: subject in left two-thirds of frame, workshop space with negative
background on right. His gloved hand and phone form the visual anchor at the
image center. 85mm lens equivalent, shallow depth of field, f/2.8.

Lighting: key light from the right at 4 o'clock position, warm tungsten from a
work lamp off-frame. Cold blue ambient from the open shop door behind him.
Classic rim light catching the edge of his helmet and shoulder. Shadows on the
left side of his face, preserved detail.

Color grading: muted industrial palette, 28% saturation, desaturated oranges
from his high-visibility vest, deep blues in shadow areas, warm copper
highlights on the phone screen edge.

Age & ethnicity: Chilean mining worker, hispanic/mestizo features, weathered
skin from outdoor work, honest working-class appearance. Late 30s to early
50s. Not cast by a model agency.

Avoid: pristine clothes, perfect lighting, app UI shown on the phone, logos,
text overlays, social media app vibes, fake dramatic poses, smiling, heroic
angle from below, "stock photo" energy.

Style reference: National Geographic portrait photography, specifically Brent
Stirton's work on industrial labor. Lighting reference: a dramatic yet honest
documentary portrait.

Resolution: 2048x2560 (portrait orientation). Format: photographic.
```

## Image 03 · Case study SIME — Production software en monitor industrial

**Uso:** case study card SIME, mostrar la realidad del software corriendo en una oficina de faena.

**Prompt para /imagine:**

```
Medium shot of an industrial-grade monitor (22" screen with thick metal bezel
suggesting ruggedized workstation) sitting on a steel desk in a cramped
mining control office at night. The monitor displays a KPI dashboard with
data tables, line graphs and work order counts — the specific content must
feel real: numbers like "19,778 work orders" and "115K activities", data
tables with dates, small bar charts. The UI should look like modern business
software (Next.js + React + TypeScript feel), NOT a sci-fi holographic display.

The desk has clutter: a coffee mug (stain ring visible), a ruggedized
clipboard with a safety inspection form, a hard hat hanging off the back of
the chair (partially visible in frame), and the corner of a paper spreadsheet.
A desk lamp on the right provides the key light, casting the computer monitor
glow across the tabletop.

Composition: the monitor occupies the right two-thirds of the frame. Negative
space on the left is the rest of the office, visible through slight
out-of-focus bokeh — suggesting a window with night outside, one or two
distant lights.

Lens: 50mm equivalent, f/4, shallow depth of field focused on the monitor
screen. The data on screen is readable but not tack-sharp (natural monitor
focus at distance).

Lighting: monitor glow provides soft cool blue ambient on the desk area,
tungsten desk lamp provides warm side light from the right at 5 o'clock, rim
light from an unseen hallway light behind the chair. Three-light setup but
looking natural, documentary.

Color grading: the monitor UI is the only saturated element in the shot — a
slightly blue-white UI color. Everything else desaturated, tungsten warmth,
green-brown shadows. 30% overall saturation.

Mood: someone has been working here for hours. Real operation, not an
advertising shoot. The chair is pushed back slightly as if someone just
walked away for a minute.

Avoid: sci-fi holographic UI, people in frame, touchscreen gestures, glossy
advertising aesthetic, perfect desk, any Apple products or premium tech
porn aesthetic, cyberpunk neon.

Style reference: cinematography from HBO's "Chernobyl" (control room
scenes), Roger Deakins lighting for "Blade Runner 2049" office interiors
(but naturalistic, not stylized sci-fi).

Resolution: 2560x1440. Format: photographic.
```

## Image 04 · About portrait — Alejandro editorial

**Uso:** sección about-founder, dentro del terminal, columna izquierda.

**Prompt para /imagine:**

```
Black and white editorial portrait of a Chilean man in his early-to-mid 40s,
three-quarter profile view, looking slightly off-camera to the right with a
focused, thoughtful expression. He wears a plain dark gray wool knit sweater
(no logos, no patterns). Clean-shaven or very short stubble. Short dark hair,
slightly gray at the temples. Honest, intelligent face — looks like an
operator, not a tech influencer. Neither posing nor performing.

Environment: seated at a wooden desk with a laptop out of focus behind him,
in what looks like a home office or a workshop space with some technical
atmosphere — perhaps a hint of technical books on a shelf out of focus, or
a mechanical drawing pinned to the wall behind him.

Composition: subject in center-right of frame, negative space on the left
for layout text. Classic editorial portrait rule of thirds. 85mm lens
equivalent, f/2.0 depth of field so the background is soft.

Lighting: single large soft key light from the right at 45 degrees, natural
fall-off into shadow on the left side of his face. Slight rim light on the
right shoulder from ambient. High-contrast black and white — deep blacks in
shadow, rich grays in midtones, preserved highlight detail on skin.

Grain: medium film grain, ISO 800 Kodak Tri-X feel.

Mood: someone who has been through enough real projects to be calm about it.
Not a founder performing confidence — someone who has earned calm by
shipping.

IMPORTANT: this is a portrait of a real person, NOT a fictional composite.
The likeness should be "generic Chilean man 40s" rather than resembling any
specific celebrity or reference.

Avoid: cheesy smile, arms crossed confidently, LinkedIn headshot vibes,
corporate suit, tech-bro aesthetic, hoodie, Silicon Valley founder clichés,
dramatic underlit angle, any saturation (this is black and white),
photoshopped smoothness, model-agency perfection.

Style reference: Richard Avedon's "In the American West" series. Editorial
portrait photography reference: Annie Leibovitz's subject-focused work for
The New Yorker.

Resolution: 2048x2560 (portrait orientation). Format: photographic, black
and white, high contrast.
```

**Nota:** este puede necesitar ajuste con una foto real de vos. Si querés, podemos pasar una foto real al /imagine con un prompt de "mantener la likeness pero aplicar este tratamiento visual". Alternativa: generar genérico y después reemplazar con la foto real cuando llegue a production.

## Image 05 · Case study Fernández — Metalurgia con iluminación dramática

**Uso:** case study card de la maestranza metalúrgica, mostrar el contexto industrial "old-school" que fue digitalizado.

**Prompt para /imagine:**

```
Wide shot of the interior of a 50-year-old Chilean industrial metalworking
workshop (maestranza) at dusk, with tall ceilings, exposed steel I-beams
overhead, and several large metalworking machines: a lathe, a milling machine,
and a welding station in the distance. The space shows its age: worn concrete
floor with oil stains, walls painted in faded industrial blue-gray, some
hand-written labels on machinery in Spanish (partially legible), scattered
metal shavings near the lathe.

Foreground: the edge of a large lathe (partial view, deep shadow). Mid-ground:
a clipboard with a paper work order resting on a metal cart, a coffee cup
beside it. Background: the welding station with a single flash of welding
spark light visible, and beyond that, a large open garage door showing the
last light of day outside — warm amber light.

NO people in frame.

Composition: strong asymmetric composition with the lathe dominant on the
left and the welding spark as a small distant focal point on the right. High
ceiling captured in the upper third with industrial lighting fixtures
visible but off.

Lens: wide angle, 24mm equivalent, f/5.6, everything in focus. High dynamic
range — preserving detail from the dark foreground to the bright welding
spark.

Lighting: the welding spark provides a small intense cool-blue point of
light. Warm tungsten from overhead work lights on the left side of the space.
Cool amber from the open garage door in the background. Mixed color
temperature deliberately preserved.

Color grading: mostly desaturated industrial tones, 25% saturation. The only
pops of color are the small welding spark (electric blue-white) and the
distant garage door light (warm amber). Everything else: grays, faded blues,
rust browns.

Mood: a workshop that has been in operation for half a century, where
real things are built by hand. Respectful of the craft. Not decay porn
(no broken windows, no abandonment), but also not corporate clean.

Avoid: modern clean factory aesthetic, CNC high-tech look, sci-fi lighting,
sparks flying everywhere for drama, heavy post-processing, HDR halos,
brand logos, any indication of Chinese mass production.

Style reference: cinematography from "There Will Be Blood" (2007) for the
industrial historical aesthetic. Photographic reference: Sebastião Salgado's
industrial series.

Resolution: 2560x1440. Format: photographic.
```

## Orden de generación recomendado

1. **Primero Image 01** (hero background). Si sale bien, sabemos que la dirección visual funciona y podemos validar las 4 restantes con el mismo treatment.
2. **Segundo Image 04** (portrait). Es la más delicada por la likeness. Si no sale bien, ajustamos el prompt.
3. **Tercero Image 02** (Schwager field worker). Es la segunda imagen más compleja por el elemento humano + tecnología.
4. **Cuarto Image 03** (SIME monitor). Puede requerir iteración porque los dashboards son difíciles de renderizar con coherencia.
5. **Quinto Image 05** (Fernández metalurgia). Interior industrial — generalmente Gemini maneja bien escenas sin sujetos humanos.

## Workflow de validación

Para cada imagen:
1. Generar con /imagine
2. Revisar si cumple: paleta desaturada ✓, composición asimétrica con negative space ✓, light direction correcta ✓, mood honesto ✓
3. Si no cumple en 2 de 4: re-prompt con ajuste específico
4. Si cumple: guardar en `design-explorations/generated/NN-nombre.png` y pasar a la siguiente
5. Tope: 3 intentos por imagen. Si a los 3 no sale, pasar a la siguiente y volver al final.

**Presupuesto:** 5 imágenes × 3 intentos máx = 15 generaciones máx para Fase 1.
